Famous Kazakhstan Delicacies

Kazakhstan's cuisine reflects its nomadic heritage, featuring hearty, meat-based dishes and dairy products.

Beshbarmak

The national dish of Kazakhstan, consisting of boiled meat (usually lamb or horse) over flat noodles.

Kazy

Traditional horse meat sausage, seasoned with salt and garlic, then smoked or dried.

Shashlik

Skewered and grilled meat, usually lamb or beef, marinated in spices and vinegar.

Kurt

Hard, salty cheese balls made from dried fermented milk, a common snack for nomads.

Baursak

Deep-fried dough pieces, often compared to donuts, served plain or with honey.

Manti

Steamed dumplings filled with minced lamb or beef, flavored with onions and black pepper.

Lagman

A noodle soup with hand-pulled noodles, beef or lamb, and a flavorful broth.

Kumis

Fermented mare’s milk, a traditional and slightly alcoholic drink.

Kumis

rich, flavorful broth made from boiled meat (often lamb or horse), served as a soup or alongside Beshbarmak. It is believed to have health benefits.
